  • Time-label format and count both adapt to the window's span, not to which preset produced it — see the table and Panel 3 below for the full rule.
  • 1D: 24-hour scale, 8 ticks, bare hour numbers (no AM/PM) — try it above.
  • 1W: 7 ticks, one weekday-initial letter per day (M/T/W/T/F/S/S).
  • 1M: 5 ticks, short dates (e.g. "Jul 19"), unchanged format from before — just one more of them.
  • 1Y: 5 ticks, month + 2-digit year (e.g. "Jul '26"), never the full 4-digit year.
  • Duration and Quality never share a value scale — each chart's 5 gridlines/labels reflect only its own min–max, recomputed independently per card.
  • An hour or weekday scale centres every label within its own slice instead of pinning it to the slice's start — so no tick sits at the plot's exact left/right edge (try "1D" or "1W" above and compare the margins to "1M"'s, which does touch both edges). This also avoids a duplicate label, since 24 hours and 7 weekdays both wrap back to their own start.
  • Resting Heart Rate stays in its axis-free "Not enough data yet" state at every range — clicking presets above never gives it gridlines.
  • Tapping "Custom" applies the same sample 5-day range shown in Custom Time Range Input's mockup; the segment's own label switches from the word "Custom" to the applied range, exactly as it does today.

CUSTOM RANGE — LABEL FORMAT IS DYNAMIC, BY SPAN

A Custom range doesn't get its own fixed format — it reuses whichever preset tier its span falls into, exactly as if that span had been a preset all along. The three ranges below are all "Custom", picked only to land in each tier.

Jul 14 – Jul 18 (5 days)
span ≤ 7 days → week style, one letter per day in range (5, not forced to 7)
Jun 5 – Jul 19 (45 days)
7 days < span ≤ ~2 months → month style, 5 short-date labels
Sep 1 '25 – Jul 19 '26 (321 days)
span > ~2 months → year style, 5 labels, month + 2-digit year
