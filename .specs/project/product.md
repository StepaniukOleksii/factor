## Product Philosophy

### The World is a Network of Factors

People constantly experience outcomes and try to understand what influences them.

Examples include:

* Sleep affecting mood.
* Exercise affecting recovery.
* Weather affecting public transport crowdiness.
* Meetings affecting productivity.
* Screen time affecting focus.
* Study habits affecting learning outcomes.

Most people rely on intuition, memory, or isolated observations to understand these relationships.

Factor is built on the belief that meaningful patterns can be discovered through systematic observation and analysis.

---

### Domain-Agnostic by Design

Factor is not a sleep tracker, mood tracker, health tracker, habit tracker, or productivity tracker.

Those are merely possible applications.

Factor treats all observations as data points that may have relationships with one another.

Users define what they want to observe.

The system helps them discover potential relationships between those observations.

Examples:

* Sleep → Mood
* Coffee Consumption → Focus
* Rainfall → Commute Duration
* Temperature → Public Transport Crowdiness
* Study Time → Exam Performance
* Stress → Physical Symptoms

The platform should not assume which relationships are important.

Users decide what questions they want to investigate.

---

### Discovery Before Assumptions

Most applications start with predefined metrics and predefined insights.

Factor takes the opposite approach.

Rather than telling users what should matter, the platform helps users discover what actually matters within their own data.

The goal is not to confirm assumptions.

The goal is to reveal patterns that may otherwise remain hidden.

---

### Data Collection Exists to Enable Insight

Collecting data is not the purpose of the product.

Data collection is a necessary step toward understanding.

Every feature should ultimately contribute to one of the following outcomes:

* Better understanding of relationships.
* Better understanding of trends.
* Better understanding of influencing factors.
* Better decision making.

If a feature does not support insight generation, it should be carefully evaluated before inclusion.

---

### Users Define Their Own Model of Reality

Every user views the world differently.

One user may be interested in health.

Another may be interested in productivity.

Another may be interested in transportation, finances, learning, or personal habits.

Factor should provide a flexible framework for recording observations and exploring relationships without imposing a predefined worldview.

The platform should adapt to the user's questions rather than forcing users into predefined categories.

---

### Correlation is a Starting Point, Not a Conclusion

Factor helps users identify potential relationships.

Insights should be presented as observations and evidence, not as unquestionable truths.

The system should encourage exploration, validation, and critical thinking.

The goal is to support understanding, not to make decisions on behalf of the user.
